Injuries & Recovery

Interactive Anatomical Reference
Muscle Soreness (DOMS)
Normal 24-72 hours after intense exercise. Rest, hydration, light stretching, foam rolling. Active recovery like walking accelerates healing. Usually resolves in 2-4 days.
Muscle Cramps
Stay hydrated, maintain electrolyte balance. Stretch gently, hold 30 seconds. Apply heat or ice. Reduce workout intensity temporarily.
Shin Splints
Rest from impact activities 2-3 weeks. Ice 15-20 minutes after activity. Strengthen calf muscles. Ensure proper running shoes with arch support.
Tendonitis
Rest 2-4 weeks, ice 3-4 times daily. Physical therapy with eccentric exercises highly beneficial. May take 6-12 weeks for full recovery.
Ankle Sprain
RICE: Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation. Avoid weight-bearing 48-72 hours. Use ankle brace on return. Most heal in 2-6 weeks.
Hamstring Strain
RICE immediately. Begin gentle stretching after 48-72 hours. Grade 1 heals in 2-3 weeks, Grade 2 in 4-8 weeks.
ACL / MCL Tear
SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION. MRI required. Do not bear weight. Rehabilitation takes 6-12 months post-surgery.
Stress Fracture
MEDICAL EVALUATION IMMEDIATELY. Complete rest 6-8 weeks minimum. Return only after medical clearance.
Severe Concussion
EMERGENCY ROOM if loss of consciousness, severe headache, or vomiting. Never return to sport without medical clearance.
⚠️
Emergency Protocol
Severe pain, loss of consciousness, difficulty breathing, chest pain, or suspected fracture — seek immediate medical attention. Do not delay.
📞 Call 102 (Ambulance) 🏥 Call 108 (Emergency)
