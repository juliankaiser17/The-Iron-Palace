Injuries & Recovery

Interactive Anatomical Reference
Muscle Soreness (DOMS)
Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness is normal 24-72 hours after intense exercise. Rest, hydration, light stretching, foam rolling, and massage help. Active recovery like walking accelerates healing. Usually resolves in 2–4 days.
Muscle Cramps
Stay hydrated and maintain electrolyte balance (sodium, potassium, magnesium). Stretch affected muscle gently and hold for 30 seconds. Apply heat or ice. Reduce workout intensity temporarily.
Shin Splints
Rest from impact activities for 2-3 weeks. Ice shins for 15-20 minutes after activity. Strengthen calf and shin muscles. Ensure proper running shoes with arch support.
Tendonitis (General)
Rest the affected area for 2-4 weeks, apply ice 3-4 times daily. Use anti-inflammatory medication as directed. Physical therapy with eccentric exercises highly beneficial. May take 6-12 weeks for full recovery.
Ankle Sprain
RICE protocol: Rest, Ice (20 min every 2-3 hours), Compression wrap, Elevate above heart. Avoid weight-bearing for 48-72 hours. Most heal in 2-6 weeks.
Hamstring Strain
RICE protocol immediately. Avoid aggressive stretching initially. Begin gentle stretching after 48-72 hours. Grade 1 strains heal in 2-3 weeks, Grade 2 in 4-8 weeks.
ACL / MCL Tear
SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION. Requires professional diagnosis via MRI and potentially surgical reconstruction. Do not exercise or bear weight. Rehabilitation takes 6-12 months post-surgery.
Stress Fracture
SEEK MEDICAL EVALUATION IMMEDIATELY. X-ray or MRI needed. Complete rest from impact activities for 6-8 weeks minimum. Gradual return to activity only after medical clearance.
Severe Concussion
EMERGENCY ROOM IMMEDIATELY if loss of consciousness, severe headache, vomiting, or confusion. Complete physical and cognitive rest. Never return to sports without medical clearance.
⚠️
Emergency Protocol
If experiencing severe pain, loss of consciousness, difficulty breathing, chest pain, suspected fracture, or severe bleeding — seek immediate medical attention. Do not delay.
Call 102 (Ambulance) Call 108 (Emergency)
